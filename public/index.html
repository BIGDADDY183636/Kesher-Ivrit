Why did Herzl believe Jews needed their own state?
Walla, great question. Herzl watched the Dreyfus trial in Paris in 1894 — a Jewish French officer falsely accused of treason, crowds screaming מָוֶת לַיְּהוּדִים — "death to the Jews." That moment broke him. He realized antisemitism wasn't going away. The only solution? A Jewish homeland. He wrote Der Judenstaat in 1896 and the world was never the same. 🇮🇱
