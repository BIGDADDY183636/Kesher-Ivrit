Hebrew · Torah · Israel · Jewish History
Why did Herzl believe Jews needed their own state?
Herzl watched the Dreyfus trial in Paris in 1894 — a Jewish officer falsely accused of treason, crowds screaming for Jewish blood. He realized antisemitism wasn't going away. The only solution was a Jewish homeland. He wrote Der Judenstaat in 1896 and changed history.
